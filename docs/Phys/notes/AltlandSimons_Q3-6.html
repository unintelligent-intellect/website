Periodic Quantum System in the Instanton Picture

Condensed Matter Field Theory > Chapter3 > Problem 3.6

In this website, I will confirm that the propagator of a quantum periodic system is compatible with the Bloch function and its spectrum.

Question

  1. A quantum mechanical particle moves in a periodic lattice potential \(V\) with period \(a\). Taking the Euclidean action for the instanton connecting two neighboring minima to be \(S_{\rm inst}\), express the Euclidean time propagator \(G(ma,na;\tau)\), with \(m\) and \(n\) integer, as a sum over instanton and anti-instanton field configurations.

  2. Making use of the identity \[\delta_{qq'} = \frac{1}{2π}\int_0^{2\pi} d\theta\, e^{i(q−q')\theta}\] show that \[ G(ma,na;\tau) \sim e^{-\omega \tau/2}\int_0^{2\pi} \frac{d\theta}{2\pi} \,e^{i(n-m)\theta} e^{\frac{\Delta \epsilon \tau}{\hbar }2\cos{\theta}}. \]

  3. Keeping in mind that, in the periodic system, the eigenfunctions are Bloch states \(\psi_{p\alpha}(q) = e^{ipq}u_{p\alpha}(q)\) where \(u_{p\alpha}(q+ma) = u_{p\alpha}(q)\) denotes the periodic part of the Bloch function, show that the propagator is compatible with a spectrum of the lowest band \[\alpha = 0,\;\epsilon_p = \frac{\hbar \omega}{2} − 2\Delta \epsilon \cos{pa}.\]

Strategy

  • Recall the formula for the instanton action \(S_{\rm inst}\) and the instanton propagator \(G_{\rm inst}\).

Solution

Propagator \(G(ma,na;\tau)\)

We learned that the most effective configurations of the Euclidean action involved consecutive sequences of instanton–anti-instanton pairs in the double-well potential. However, in the periodic system, because of the boundary condition, the difference in the number of instantons and anti-instantons must be congruent to the number of potential units passed. Let the number of instantons be \(q\) and let the number of anti-instantons be \(q'\), then \(q-q'=n-m\) must be satisfied.

Recall the formula for the propagator in the double-well potential. In this case, the calculation can be done simply by changing the way instantons are taken. First, approximate the contribution from staying in the bottom of the potential \(\sqrt{1/\sinh{\omega\tau}}\simeq e^{-\omega\tau/2}\) (instanton gas). The contribution from \(n\) (anti-)instantons can be written as \[G(\mathrm{[path]};\tau)\sim \sum_{n:\mathrm{allowed}} \frac{\left(\tau K e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)^n}{n!}.\]

\(n\) is the number of instantons and anti-instantons, but what we should take into account is the particle can move along the path in \(n\) steps. For ease, we neglect this annoying fact until we get the complete form of the propagator.

The contribution from \(q\) instantons and \(q'\) anti-instantons can be written as \[G(ma,na;\tau)\sim \sum_{q,q':\mathrm{allowed}} \frac{\left(\tau K e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)^{q+q'}}{q!q'!}.\]

By considering all of the aforementioned factors, we obtain the following

\[G(ma,na;\tau)\sim e^{-\omega \tau/2}\sum_{q,q':\mathrm{allowed}} \frac{\left(\tau K e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)^{q+q'}}{q!q'!}.\]

Finally, we limit the index of sum:

\[G(ma,na;\tau)\sim e^{-\omega \tau/2}\sum_{q,q'}\delta_{q-q',n-m} \frac{\left(\tau K e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)^{q+q'}}{q!q'!}.\]

Rewriting the propagator

Substituing \(\delta_{q-q',m-n}\) for \(\delta_{q-q',m-n} = \frac{1}{2π}\int_0^{2\pi} d\theta\, e^{i(q−q'-n+m)\theta}\), we obtain the following result:

\[ \begin{aligned} G(ma,na;\tau) &\sim e^{-\omega \tau/2}\sum_{q,q'}\int_0^{2\pi} \frac{d\theta}{2\pi} \,e^{i(q-q'-n+m)\theta} \frac{\left(\tau K e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)^{q+q'}}{q!q'!}\\ &\sim e^{-\omega \tau/2}\int_0^{2\pi} \frac{d\theta}{2\pi} \,e^{-i(n-m)\theta} \sum_{q}\frac{\left(\tau K e^{i\theta}e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)^{q}}{q!}\sum_{q'}\frac{\left(\tau K e^{-i\theta}e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)^{q'}}{q'!}\\ &\sim e^{-\omega \tau/2}\int_0^{2\pi} \frac{d\theta}{2\pi} \,e^{-i(n-m)\theta} \exp{\left(\tau K e^{i\theta}e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)}\exp{\left(\tau K e^{-i\theta}e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)}\\ \end{aligned} \] Let \(\Delta \epsilon:=\hbar K \exp{\left(-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar \right)}\). Then: \[ \begin{aligned} G(ma,na;\tau) &\sim e^{-\omega \tau/2}\int_0^{2\pi} \frac{d\theta}{2\pi} \,e^{-i(n-m)\theta} \exp{\left(\tau K e^{i\theta}e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)}\exp{\left(\tau K e^{-i\theta}e^{-S_\mathrm{inst}/\hbar}\right)}\\ &\sim e^{-\omega \tau/2}\int_0^{2\pi} \frac{d\theta}{2\pi} \,e^{-i(n-m)\theta} \exp{\left(\frac{\Delta \epsilon\tau}{\hbar} e^{i\theta}\right)}\exp{\left(\frac{\Delta \epsilon\tau}{\hbar}e^{-i\theta}\right)}\\ &\sim \int_0^{2\pi} \frac{d\theta}{2\pi} \,e^{-i(n-m)\theta} \exp{\left(-\omega \tau/2+2\frac{\Delta \epsilon\tau}{\hbar} \cos{\theta}\right)} \end{aligned} \]

Compatibility with the Bloch theory

Assume the system is periodic and Hamiltonian satisfies \(H|p\rangle=\epsilon_p|p\rangle\). The original definition of the propagator is \(G(ma,na,\tau)=\langle na|e^{-\tau H/\hbar}|ma\rangle\). In the words of Bloch’s theory, the propagator is written as follows:

\[ \begin{aligned} G(ma,na,t)&=\langle na|e^{-\tau H/\hbar}|ma\rangle\\ &=\sum_p \langle na|p\rangle e^{-\tau \epsilon_p/\hbar}\langle p|ma\rangle\\ &=\sum_p \psi_p(na) e^{-\tau \epsilon_p/\hbar}\psi^*_p(ma)\\ &=\sum_p |\psi_p(0)|^2e^{-\tau \epsilon_p/\hbar+ip\left(n-m\right)a}\\ \end{aligned} \]

The correspondence with the results of the previous problem is as follows:

\[ \begin{aligned} \int \,dp&\leftrightarrow \sum_p\\ \theta &\leftrightarrow -pa\\ \epsilon_p &\leftrightarrow \frac{\hbar \omega}{2}-2\Delta \epsilon\cos{pa}\\ \end{aligned} \]
